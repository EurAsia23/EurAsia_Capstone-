Root cause #1

Sleep deprivation is the leading cause of the decline in student health because the biological sleep pattern in adolescents causes students to sleep later and wake up later.
Implication: This impact is the nationwide call for later school times, as research shows that students need more sleep. Also, bring to attention the importance of talking to and understanding students other than the surface level of just their attendance.
There is ample research available on the biology of sleep, specifically about adolescents. This body of research details adolescents' unique circadian rhythms, patterns of later melatonin release, and bio-regulatory processes (delayed phase preference) associated with later evening sleepiness, Even a moderate increase in sleep (30 minutes to one hour) over a period of time has shown to be associated with increased scores on various performance tasks (e.g., reaction times and memory tests) for youth;
This evidence supports my claim because students' bodies release melatonin, a chemical that makes people tired and is produced later than adults or children. This causes students to fall asleep later, and the early school start times interpret the natural times that students would wake up, causing sleep deprivation.
Studies have shown that sleep deprivation can lead to metabolic and cognitive disturbances in brain areas involved in learning, memory, and emotion, such as the hippocampus, amygdala, and prefrontal cortex (Havekes et al., 2012).
